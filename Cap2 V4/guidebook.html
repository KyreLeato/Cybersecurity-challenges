Phishing Awareness

What is Phishing?

Phishing involves fraudulent emails, texts, or websites that trick users into sharing sensitive information. Always verify the sender's identity and avoid clicking suspicious links. Look for signs like spelling errors or urgent requests.

Watch: Phishing Explained

Consequences

  • Loss of sensitive data (passwords, financial info)
  • Identity theft
  • Financial loss

Remediation

  • Do not click suspicious links or attachments
  • Verify sender identity before responding
  • Report phishing attempts to your IT/security team

Password Security

Password Security

Use strong, unique passwords for each account. A good password is a long passphrase, like "blue-sky-rainbow-2023". Never reuse passwords or share them with anyone, even trusted individuals.

Watch: Password Security Tips

Consequences

  • Accounts can be easily hacked if passwords are weak or reused
  • Personal and financial data theft
  • Loss of access to important services

Remediation

  • Use a password manager to generate and store strong passwords
  • Change passwords regularly
  • Never share your passwords with anyone

Safe Browsing

Safe Browsing

Ensure websites use "https://" and display a padlock icon for secure connections. Be cautious of sites requesting unnecessary permissions and avoid downloading from untrusted sources.

Watch: Safe Browsing Explained

Consequences

  • Exposure to malware and viruses
  • Phishing attacks and data theft
  • Compromised personal and financial information

Remediation

  • Always check for "https://" and the padlock icon
  • Do not download files from untrusted sources
  • Keep your browser and antivirus software updated

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

2FA adds an extra layer of security by requiring a second form of verification, like a code sent to your phone. Enable it on all accounts that offer it to protect against unauthorized access.

Watch: What is 2FA?

Consequences

  • Accounts are more vulnerable to hacking without 2FA
  • Unauthorized access to sensitive data
  • Potential identity theft

Remediation

  • Enable 2FA on all accounts that support it
  • Use authentication apps or SMS codes for verification
  • Never share your 2FA codes with anyone
